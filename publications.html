Contributions to Science

A thematic overview of my research contributions. A complete chronological list follows below.

  1. 1

    Development of conversational AI systems for mental health

    I develop and rigorously evaluate large language models for therapeutic dialogue. This effort includes constructing MentalChat16K, a 16,000-example benchmark of counseling conversations, and instruction-tuning open models (LLaMA-2, Mistral, Vicuna, and Zephyr) that improved over their base counterparts across seven clinically grounded metrics. My contributions span dataset curation and synthetic-data generation, model fine-tuning, and the design of dual human-expert and LLM-as-judge evaluation protocols that support the safe, structured deployment of conversational AI in mental-health and dementia-care settings.

    1. Xu J, Wei T, Hou B, Orzechowski P, Yang S, Jin R, Paulbeck R, Wagenaar J, Demiris G, Shen L. MentalChat16K: A benchmark dataset for conversational mental health assistance. Proceedings of the 31st ACM SIGKDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining V.2; 2025. DOI
    2. Xu J, Wei T, Hou B, Demiris G, Shen L. Revolutionizing Dementia Care: Enhancing Talk Therapy with Fine-Tuned Large Language Models Using GPT Self-Generated Data. Alzheimer’s & Dementia. 2024;20:e093496. Link
  2. 2

    Identification of determinants of biological aging using phenotypic age modeling

    I investigate the determinants of biological aging through phenotypic-age modeling on large population cohorts, including NHANES. By jointly modeling socioeconomic disparity, environmental exposures, and biological markers, this line of work characterizes the mechanisms underlying the gap between phenotypic and chronological age. I contributed to analytic design, feature selection, and statistical modeling across mediation analysis, structural equation modeling, and causal-inference frameworks, as well as to the interpretation of the resulting pathways.

    1. Pala D, Xie Y, Xu J, Shen L. Modeling the impact of socioeconomic disparity, biological markers and environmental exposures on phenotypic age using mediation analysis and structural equation models. International Journal of Medical Informatics. 2025;193:105661. DOI
    2. Pala D, Xu J, Xie Y, Zhang Y, Shen L. Identifying biological markers and sociodemographic factors that influence the gap between phenotypic and chronological ages. Informatics for Health and Social Care. 2024;49(3-4):162-76. Link
    3. Pala D, Xie Y, Xu J, Zhang Y, Shen L. Causal Effects of Environmental Exposures and Biological Traits on the Difference between Phenotypic and Chronological Ages. 2023 IEEE International Conference on Bioinformatics and Biomedicine (BIBM); 2023: IEEE. Link
    4. Xu J, Xie Y, Zhang Y, Pala D. Identification of the Most Determinant Demographic and Biological Factors for the Phenotypic and Chronological Age Difference Prediction in the NHANES Cohort. AMIA 2023 Informatics Summit.
  3. 3

    Machine learning approaches for Alzheimer’s disease subtype discovery

    I apply representation learning to uncover latent heterogeneity in Alzheimer’s disease. Using similarity learning together with graph-diffusion methods, this work recovers disease subtypes that enable finer-grained patient stratification for neurodegenerative disorders. I contributed to model development, implementation, and the evaluation of subtype stability and clinical relevance.

    1. Wei T, Yang S, Tarzanagh DA, Bao J, Xu J, Orzechowski P, Wagenaar JB, Long Q, Shen L. Clustering Alzheimer’s Disease Subtypes via Similarity Learning and Graph Diffusion. arXiv preprint arXiv:2410.03937. 2024. arXiv
  4. 4

    Biomedical signal processing and quantitative modeling

    My earlier work established a quantitative and signal-processing foundation for my subsequent machine-learning research. It comprises an adaptive ECG-denoising method based on CEEMDAN and a closed-form analysis of magnetohydrodynamic flow past an oscillating cylindrical surface. I carried out the mathematical modeling, algorithm implementation, and empirical validation for these studies.

    1. Wu Y, Xu J. Simplified analysis of MHD flow in a porous surrounding bounded by an oscillating vertical cylindrical surface. Case Studies in Thermal Engineering. 2022;30:101737.
    2. Zhao Y, Xu J. Denoising of ECG signals based on CEEMDAN. 2021 6th International Conference on Intelligent Computing and Signal Processing (ICSP); 2021: IEEE.
  5. 5

    Fairness-oriented multiview learning for health data

    I contribute to fairness-aware multiview representation learning for biomedical data. This work introduces Fair Canonical Correlation Analysis, which embeds fairness constraints directly into the CCA objective while preserving cross-view correlation structure. I contributed to method formulation, theoretical analysis, and empirical validation toward more equitable computational tools.

    1. Zhou Z, Ataee Tarzanagh D, Hou B, Tong B, Xu J, Feng Y, Long Q, Shen L. Fair canonical correlation analysis. Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS 2023 Main Track). 2023;36:3675-705. Paper
